Famous Authors

The first half of the 20th century liberated the written word

Authors unshackled themselves from as much structure as possible and searched for new forms of expression

Much of this was in Europe with the backdrop of the two wars and the great depression

E.E. Cummings

Cummings decided to become a poet when he was still a child.

Between the ages of eight and twenty-two, he wrote a poem a day.

Cummings also experimented with poems as visual objects on the page.

It was with these collections of the 1920s that Cummings established his reputation as an avant-garde poet conducting daring experiments with language.

An example of the visual poetic style of ee cummings.

How does one write like E.E. Cummings?

  • use case when it matters
  • think about white-space as a semantic part of the document
  • integrate the rhythym of the prose with the depth of the content

Albert Camus

Author around the second World War.

He is considered an Absurdist writer.

During the war Camus joined the French Resistance cell Combat, which published an underground newspaper of the same name.

Camus became the paper's editor in 1943.

Died in 1960.

What order should his works be read in?

    1. Caligula
    2. The Misunderstanding
    3. The State of Siege
    1. The Stranger
    2. The Plague
    3. The Fall

In philosophy, "the Absurd" refers to the conflict between the human tendency to seek inherent value and meaning in life and the human inability to find any.
